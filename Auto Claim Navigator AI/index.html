Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I recover from a total loss dispute?

Most clients recover $3,000-8,000 on total loss disputes. The average undervaluation is $4,200, with 77% of initial offers below actual cash value. Luxury and specialty vehicles often see $10,000+ increases.

What is diminished value and can I claim it?

Diminished value is the loss in your vehicle's worth after repairs. Recovery ranges from $2,000-15,000. Most states allow DV claims within 3-6 years of the accident. Georgia, North Carolina, and Kansas have the strongest DV laws.

Do I have the right to OEM parts?

Vehicles under 3 years old or with less than 36,000 miles typically qualify for OEM parts. Many states require insurers to guarantee aftermarket parts match OEM quality. Always demand OEM for safety components and ADAS sensors.

How long do I have to appeal?

Most policies require appeals within 30-60 days of denial. State laws vary: California allows 2 years, Texas has 2 years, Florida gives 5 years for DV claims. Don't wait - evidence becomes harder to obtain over time.

What if my frame was damaged?

Frame damage significantly impacts value and safety. Document all structural repairs, demand manufacturer-approved procedures, and calculate 20-50% diminished value. Many insurers try to hide frame damage - we expose it.
